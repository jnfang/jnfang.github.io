About PBHA SUP

Phillips Brooks House Association (PBHA) is Harvard’s umbrella organization for every kind of community service that occurs on campus. The Summer Urban Program (SUP) is a set of affordable summer camps held by PBHA for local kids to have continued academic and experiential enrichment. One of these camps is the Chinatown Adventure or CHAD program which seeks to offer seven weeks of  summer programming to young people ages six to 13.

My inspiration

My grandfather has always been an inspiration to me. He ran 8 km every day, whether it is rain or shine and consistently does so at his home in a village near Yixing China. He has done so for the past 10 years, every single day, since he was first  diagnosed with type II diabetes.

A month and a half ago, he ran his last 8 km ever and passed away from a stroke later that day.  The last time I saw him was four years ago, the summer before I moved to Boston and I was planning to see him this summer. I had been telling him about my training and I know he would proud to hear that I will have completed my first marathon this April.
Anna (5th grader that I am a counselor for) and I at Halloween at Harvard, an annual event put on my Chinatown Afterschool
I am a counselor for the Chinatown Afterschool Program during the semester and many of my six-graders have gone to SUP in Chinatown for the many years. It means a lot to their families to have an affordable, reliable, and educational summer camp for their kids.
